History of Viswanathan Anand

Early Life

Viswanathan Anand was born on December 11, 1969, in Chennai, India. He showed an interest in chess from a very young age, learning the game at the age of six. Anand was coached by his mother, Susheela, and soon began to participate in local tournaments.

Career Highlights

Anand became the youngest Indian to achieve the title of Grandmaster in 1988. His breakthrough came in 2000 when he won the FIDE World Chess Championship. Anand is known for his speed and versatility in various chess formats, including rapid and blitz chess.

World Chess Champion

Viswanathan Anand held the title of World Chess Champion from 2007 to 2013. He successfully defended his title in 2008, 2010, and 2012. Anand is revered for his contributions to chess and his influence in popularizing the game in India.

Recent Achievements

In recent years, Anand has continued to compete at the highest level and remains a prominent figure in the chess world. He has also been involved in promoting chess through various initiatives and has authored several books on the game.

Legacy

Viswanathan Anand's legacy extends beyond his chess accomplishments. He has been an inspiration to many young chess players in India and around the world. His achievements have earned him numerous accolades and respect in the international chess community.
